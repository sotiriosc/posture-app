# Praxis for Gyms: Product Identity

This document defines the correct orientation for the local gym SaaS product. It is the first layer of transformation: purpose, audience, promise, boundaries, and proof.

## Product identity

**Praxis for Gyms is an equipment-aware member guidance system for fitness facilities.**

It helps a gym turn its actual floor, equipment, trainers, and coaching standards into a clearer member experience.

It is not a generic AI workout app.
It is not a replacement for trainers.
It is not gym management software.
It is not a sales funnel disguised as coaching.

It is a structured bridge between:

```txt
gym equipment
member goals
trainer standards
clear programming
honest personal training guidance
```

## Core directive

> Help every member know what to do next, understand why it matters, and feel supported by the gym they belong to.

That is the SaaS identity.

## Primary audience

The first buyer is the local gym owner or operator.

They care about:

- Member confidence
- Trial conversion
- Retention
- Facility usage
- Trainer consistency
- Personal training revenue
- Differentiation from other gyms

The first user is the member.

They care about:

- What do I do today?
- Am I doing the right thing?
- Which equipment should I use?
- What if the machine is busy?
- How do I progress safely?
- When should I ask for help?

The first internal beneficiary is the trainer.

They care about:

- Clear coaching standards
- Less random programming
- Better prepared clients
- More trust before the sales conversation
- Better client education
- Less pressure to oversell

## The value triangle

Praxis for Gyms must serve all three sides at once:

```txt
Member
  clarity, confidence, progression

Trainer
  consistency, education, stronger coaching bridge

Owner
  retention, engagement, PT conversion, brand trust
```

If a feature only serves one side while weakening another, it is not correctly oriented.

## Product promise

### To the member

> You will not be left wandering. Your gym has a plan for you.

### To the trainer

> The system supports your craft by giving members better context, better questions, and a more consistent foundation.

### To the owner

> Your facility becomes easier to use, easier to trust, and easier to grow into personal training.

## First proof of identity

The product proves itself when a local gym can say:

> A new member can walk in, answer a few questions, and receive a plan that uses our actual equipment, explains the purpose, and points them toward support when they need it.

The MVP should prove this before anything else.

## MVP identity test

The first version should answer these questions clearly:

1. What gym is this for?
2. What equipment does this location have?
3. What kind of member is being served?
4. What program did the system create?
5. Why does the program fit this gym?
6. Where does a trainer add value?
7. Why would the owner pay for this?

If the demo cannot answer these, it is not ready for outreach.

## Correct orientation of screens

### Sales/demo landing page

Purpose: prove the business case.

It should show:

- What the product is
- Who it serves
- Why equipment-aware programming matters
- How it supports trainers
- Why it helps retention and PT conversion

### Owner/admin page

Purpose: prove operational fit.

It should show:

- Gym profile
- Equipment inventory
- Brand setup
- Invite/member onboarding flow
- Usage and opportunity signals

### Member page

Purpose: prove user value.

It should show:

- Simple onboarding snapshot
- Program generated around gym equipment
- Substitution logic
- Education cards
- Clear next session
- Trainer support prompt when needed

### Trainer page

Purpose: prove trainer alignment.

It should show:

- Generated plan review
- Shared cue library
- Client readiness notes
- Coaching opportunities
- Honest support language

## Boundaries

Do not build these first:

- Class scheduling
- Payroll
- Door access
- Complex CRM
- Full nutrition platform
- Full trainer marketplace
- Full native app
- Payment processing for gym memberships

Those distract from identity.

Build this first:

```txt
gym profile
location equipment map
member onboarding
program preview
substitution preview
trainer support layer
owner value metrics
```

## Tone

The product should sound:

- Professional
- Calm
- Trustworthy
- Clear
- Education-first
- Honest about trainer value
- Confident without hype

Avoid:

- Overclaiming AI magic
- Sounding like trainers are obsolete
- Manipulative upsell language
- Mystical language in operational screens
- Generic SaaS buzzwords

## The sentence that should guide the product

> Praxis for Gyms gives every member a clear, equipment-aware path through the facility while helping trainers and owners serve them better.

That is the identity to prove first.
